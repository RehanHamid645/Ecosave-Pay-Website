## What Is a Smart Meter?
A smart meter is a digital device that records your gas and electricity usage and automatically shares this information with your energy supplier. The usage data is also displayed on a small in-home screen, showing you in real time how much energy youâ€™re using and how much itâ€™s costing.
This visibility helps you spot areas where you can cut back and make smarter energy decisions.
## Benefits of Smart Meters
- **Keep energy costs down**

    Smart meters show your daily gas and electricity spend, so you can see where costs are highest and adjust your usage to save money.

- **Accurate bills**

    No more estimated bills. Smart meters provide exact readings, ensuring you only pay for the energy you use.

- **Data protection**

    Smart meters do not store personal details, they only track energy usage data.

- **Better budgeting**

    Real-time insights make it easier to set budgets and manage cash flow.





## Are Smart Meters Free?
Yes. Smart meters are provided and installed free of charge by your energy supplier. The installation is straightforward and comes at no additional cost.
## How Long Does Installation Take?
Installing a smart meter typically takes around 30 minutes. During this time, your gas and electricity may be switched off briefly.
## Do Smart Meters Need Wi-Fi?
No. Smart meters connect through a secure national wireless network, not your home or business Wi-Fi.
## Can Smart Meters Be Hacked or Spy on You?
No. Smart meters cannot spy on you. They only record energy usage data, which is encrypted and sent securely to your supplier. Modern smart meters have strict cybersecurity protections in line with government standards.
## Are Smart Meters Mandatory?
Smart meters are not mandatory in the UK. However, many suppliers encourage them because:
- They make billing more accurate.
- They help customers understand usage better.
- Some suppliers offer incentives or lower tariffs if you install one.

## Can You Switch Suppliers with a Smart Meter?
Yes. If you have a SMETS2 smart meter, you can switch suppliers without needing a replacement.
With older SMETS1 smart meters, some customers experienced compatibility issues when switching, meaning the meter reverted to a traditional function. However, many SMETS1 devices are now being remotely upgraded to work like SMETS2.

## Can Renters Get a Smart Meter?
Yes, if you pay your own energy bills. You should notify your landlord or letting agent before installation and check your tenancy agreement first.
